Key concepts from the day

The ideas participants found most useful, distilled from the talk and the live demos.

The Memento analogy

LLMs have no memory across sessions. Like Leonard in Memento, everything they need to know must be "tattooed" into the context window. When context fills up, the model confidently contradicts what you discussed 30 minutes ago. The fix: kill the old session, start a new one.

Intelligence as electricity

Training is a sunk cost (billions). Inference is the marginal cost (cents per million tokens, falling 10x per year). The competitive fringe is real — open-weight models trail by ~4 months. Pick models by task, not by brand. Lock-in is the risk.

The escape–enter loop

The core workflow: give an instruction, watch the agent work, hit Escape when it goes wrong, course-correct, hit Enter again. Never let a toxic context linger — start fresh. Never let the same session verify its own work.

Unbundle the stack

Five separable layers: model, harness, tools, MCP, skills. You can swap any one without touching the others. The harness is a commodity. The model is a commodity. Your structure and your skills are the durable investment.
