What this shows

  1. Every model got the Stata right — from Opus 4.8 ($5/M input) to Gemma 4 ($0.06/M input), all 13 working models produced valid collapse or tabstat code. The free 1.2B model failed.
  2. Cost varies 100x for the same answer — Gemma 4 cost 0.005¢. Kimi K2.6 cost 1.01¢. Gemini 3.5 Flash cost 0.91¢. DeepSeek V4 Flash cost 0.01¢. Sonnet 4.5 cost 0.19¢. The expensive models are not better at this task.
  3. Speed varies 24x at the frontier — Gemini 3.1 Pro delivered 924 t/s. Kimi K2.6 delivered 44. Mercury 2 (diffusion LLM) hit 710 t/s at a fraction of the cost. For agent loops, speed compounds.
  4. Reasoning is not always helpful — Kimi K2.6 spent 2,400 tokens deliberating whether to include use, then omitted it. Grok 4.20 spent 2,226 tokens to produce tabstat. Sonnet 4.5 spent 56 tokens and got it right.
